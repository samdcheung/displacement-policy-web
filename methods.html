Method

The mapping is a qualitative governance assessment based on publicly available laws, policies, institutional arrangements, and secondary research.

It is not a country ranking, performance index, or scorecard.

The objective is to identify patterns of governance capacity, institutional fragmentation, and opportunities for reform.

01

Governance Fault Lines

Categorical, temporal and territorial fault lines identify where displaced populations lose access to ordinary systems.

02

Fiscal Architecture

Budget integration, adaptive systems and public expenditure arrangements show whether inclusion can become a sustained public commitment.

03

Infrastructure Governance

Safeguards, resettlement systems, registry integration and post-project continuity assess whether project-induced displacement connects to national systems.

04

Reform Pathways

Priority reforms identify feasible counterpart institutions and practical shifts toward sustainable inclusion.
