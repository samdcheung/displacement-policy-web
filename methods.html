Methods

Diagnostic Framework

The mapping framework is qualitative and analytical. It supports comparative policy analysis, not ranking.

Mapping Method

Displacement Governance Mapping draws from publicly available laws, policies, institutional frameworks, published reports, and the underlying Cheung displacement continuum / sustainable inclusion framework.

It does not claim to be an original statistical dataset. It is designed to visualize governance architecture, institutional fault lines, and reform pathways across countries and displacement dynamics.

Assessments are qualitative analytical judgements and should be updated manually as new sources become available.

01

Governance Fault Lines

Categorical, temporal and territorial fault lines identify where displaced populations lose access to ordinary systems.

02

Fiscal Architecture

Budget integration, adaptive systems and recurrent obligation risk show whether inclusion can become a sustained public commitment.

03

Infrastructure Governance

Safeguards, resettlement systems, registry integration and post-project continuity assess whether project-induced displacement connects to national systems.

04

Reform Pathways

Priority reforms identify feasible counterpart institutions and practical shifts toward sustainable inclusion.

Displacement Policy examines how states and institutions carry displacement as a recurrent public obligation.
