Mapping Methodology

A qualitative governance assessment for comparing how displacement responsibility is organised, financed, and sustained across public systems.

Mapping Method

Displacement Governance Mapping is a qualitative governance assessment based on publicly available laws, policies, institutional arrangements, published reports, academic literature, and comparative governance analysis.

It is not a country ranking, performance index, scorecard, statistical dataset, or original field data collection. The objective is to identify patterns of governance capacity, institutional fragmentation, and opportunities for reform.

The methodology has global applicability. Southeast Asia is the current regional application of the framework.

01

Governance Fault Lines

Categorical, temporal and territorial fault lines identify where displaced populations lose access to ordinary systems.

02

Fiscal Architecture

Budget integration, adaptive systems and public expenditure arrangements show whether inclusion can become a sustained public commitment.

03

Infrastructure Governance

Safeguards, resettlement systems, registry integration and post-project continuity assess whether project-induced displacement connects to national systems.

04

Reform Pathways

Priority reforms identify feasible counterpart institutions and practical shifts toward sustainable inclusion.

Displacement Policy examines how states and institutions carry displacement as a recurrent public obligation.
