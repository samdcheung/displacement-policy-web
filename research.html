Research

Understanding displacement governance.

Displacement is often approached as a humanitarian challenge. This research programme examines displacement as a governance challenge: how responsibility is organised, financed, and sustained across institutions over time.

Research Programme

The research draws on governance, public administration, social protection, public finance, and development policy to examine how states respond when displacement becomes a recurrent public obligation rather than a temporary emergency.

Core Concepts

Framework

The Displacement Continuum

Samuel Cheung | June 2026

Displacement is rarely a single event. People move across legal categories, institutions, territories, and time horizons. The framework identifies where responsibility breaks down, where institutions stop following people, and where protection and assistance become fragmented.

Framework

Fiscal Architecture of Displacement

Samuel Cheung | June 2026

This framework examines how budgets, social protection programmes, public expenditure systems, and development finance shape responses to displacement as a question of fiscal governance.

Concept

Institutional Absorption Capacity

Samuel Cheung | June 2026

This research examines the capacity of institutions and public systems to absorb displaced populations through existing governance arrangements rather than parallel structures.

Concept

Sustainable Inclusion

Samuel Cheung | June 2026

Long-term solutions depend on whether displaced populations can access services, livelihoods, rights, and opportunities through ordinary public systems.

From Framework to Application

The concepts developed through this research are applied through Displacement Governance Mapping. The current phase focuses on Southeast Asia as the first regional application of the methodology.
