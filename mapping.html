Southeast Asia Displacement Governance Mapping

A comparative methodology for analysing how states govern displacement.

Displacement Governance Mapping examines how displacement is governed through legal frameworks, institutions, public systems, fiscal architecture, and development finance.

Rather than focusing only on displacement outcomes, the project examines how responsibility is organised, where governance systems absorb displacement-related vulnerability, and where governance challenges emerge.

The current phase focuses on Southeast Asia as the first regional application of the methodology.

What the Mapping Examines

Legal frameworks, institutional responsibility, public systems, finance, and inclusion.

  • Legal Frameworks
  • Institutional Responsibility
  • Social Protection Systems
  • Fiscal Architecture
  • Development Finance Exposure
  • Sustainable Inclusion

Southeast Asia

Method

Method

The mapping is a qualitative governance assessment based on publicly available laws, policies, institutional arrangements, and secondary research.

It is not a country ranking, performance index, or scorecard.

The objective is to identify patterns of governance capacity, institutional fragmentation, and opportunities for reform.
