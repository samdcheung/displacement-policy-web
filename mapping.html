Mapping

A structured view of responsibility, systems, and fault lines.

This mapping examines governance, institutional responsibility, and fiscal authority across multiple displacement-related sectors. It shows where responsibility is assigned, where public systems absorb displacement-related vulnerability, and where governance breaks across categories, territories, and time.

The purpose is not to score countries. It is to make visible the institutional fault lines that determine whether displaced populations remain connected to ordinary systems after status, location, crisis phase, or project boundaries change.

How to use it

Use the explorer below as one mapping tool. Select by country, governance layer, or fault line; illustrative cases and sources then appear in context rather than as a separate catalogue.

Overall view

What the mapping shows.

The mapping is organised around three ways of reading the same dataset: country profiles, governance layers, and fault lines. Illustrative cases and sources appear within each view.

Profiles

Country-level governance summaries

Each profile summarises the main continuity problem and how public systems, mandates, data, and finance interact.

Layers

Seven governance layers

The same dimensions are applied across countries so systems can be compared without turning the mapping into a ranking.

Fault lines

Three forms of discontinuity

Categorical, territorial, and temporal breaks show where responsibility changes even when vulnerability continues.

Cases

Evidence in context

Cases are not a separate catalogue; they are attached to the country, layer, or fault line where they explain the governance problem.
