Mapping Project

Southeast Asia Displacement Governance Mapping

A qualitative governance database and visualization tool for tracing how public systems recognise, absorb, or lose displaced populations.

Database

From research framework to comparative governance map.

The mapping translates the Displacement Continuum, Sustainable Inclusion, and Fiscal Architecture frameworks into a structured regional database. It shows where responsibility is assigned, where public systems absorb displacement-related vulnerability, and where governance breaks across categories, territories, and time.

It is a framework-derived qualitative assessment, not a ranking, scorecard, or statistical index.

How to read it

Explore by country, compare one governance area across all countries, or trace chapter cases such as HICS, Palu, Marawi, Xayaburi, and Lower Sesan 2 through the framework.

Method

Qualitative governance assessment

The database is designed to make interpretive assumptions visible rather than hide them behind scores.

Source base

Drawn from the book chapter, the Governing Displacement briefs, secondary research, and practitioner analysis.

Cell structure

Each cell contains a characterization, anchor mechanisms, primary fault line, governance pattern, and confidence level.

What it is not

Not a country ranking, quantitative index, monitoring tracker, or exhaustive legal database.

The mapping is strongest where chapter cases and institutional mechanisms are well documented. Lower-confidence cells are retained because they identify where further research, sourcing, or field validation would strengthen the database.

This tool operationalizes the Displacement Continuum and Sustainable Inclusion frameworks developed on the Research page and in the Governing Displacement series.
