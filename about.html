About

Displacement Policy is a platform for research, analysis, and dialogue on displacement governance.

About

The platform explores how states, institutions, and public systems respond to displacement across humanitarian, development, and climate contexts. Its focus is not only on displacement events themselves, but on the governance arrangements through which responsibility for displaced populations is organised, financed, and sustained over time.

Displacement is often studied through separate lenses: refugee protection, internal displacement, migration, humanitarian response, disaster risk management, development, social protection, or climate adaptation. While each provides important insights, policy and institutional responses are frequently fragmented across sectors, mandates, and time horizons.

Displacement Policy seeks to bring these conversations into closer dialogue through a governance lens. The platform examines how legal frameworks, public institutions, fiscal systems, social protection programmes, and development policies interact in shaping displacement outcomes.

The work is organised around several core areas of research:

  • Governance and institutional responsibility
  • Fiscal architecture and public finance
  • Social protection and sustainable inclusion
  • State capacity and institutional adaptation
  • Regional and comparative approaches to displacement governance

The platform's research includes conceptual frameworks, policy analysis, comparative governance mapping, and applied research on displacement systems in Southeast Asia and beyond.

While the platform addresses displacement governance globally, current comparative research focuses on Southeast Asia as the first regional application of its governance mapping methodology.

Founder

Displacement Policy was established by Samuel Cheung.

Samuel has worked across headquarters, regional, and field contexts on refugee protection, internal displacement, mixed migration, durable solutions, and humanitarian coordination. His experience includes senior leadership and policy roles within UNHCR, including serving as Chief of Protection from Violence and Displacement, Chief of Internal Displacement, and Global Protection Cluster Coordinator.

His work has focused on the governance of displacement across humanitarian, development, and peacebuilding contexts, including institutional reform, protection systems, durable solutions, social protection, and displacement policy.

Policy Experience

The platform draws on experience across global policy development, operational leadership, and inter-agency coordination on displacement and protection.

Areas of work have included:

  • Protection leadership in humanitarian crises
  • Internal displacement policy and solutions
  • Global protection coordination
  • Durable solutions and institutional reform
  • Social protection and inclusion approaches
  • Humanitarian-development collaboration on displacement

This experience informs the platform's focus on governance, institutions, public systems, and long-term responses to displacement.

Selected policy contributions have included leadership and authorship roles in global policy and strategic initiatives relating to protection, internal displacement, humanitarian coordination, and durable solutions.

Looking Forward

Displacement Policy reflects a growing recognition that many contemporary displacement challenges are ultimately questions of governance: how responsibility is allocated, how institutions adapt, how public systems absorb displacement-related vulnerability, and how sustainable responses are financed over time.

The platform is intended as a space for research, analysis, and collaboration at the intersection of displacement and governance.

Displacement Policy examines how states and institutions carry displacement as a recurrent public obligation.
