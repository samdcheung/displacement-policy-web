About Displacement Policy

A research platform examining displacement through the lens of governance: how responsibility is organised, financed, and sustained over time.

Core focus

Governance architecture, institutional adaptation, fiscal systems, social protection, and sustainable inclusion.

Platform

How public systems respond when displacement becomes a recurrent governance challenge.

Displacement Policy explores how states, institutions, and public systems respond to displacement across humanitarian, development, and climate contexts.

Its focus is not only on displacement itself, but on the governance arrangements through which responsibility for displaced populations is allocated across laws, institutions, public systems, and financing arrangements.

The platform brings together conversations that are often treated separately: refugee protection, internal displacement, migration, humanitarian response, disaster risk management, development, social protection, and climate adaptation.

Research Focus

Current areas of work.

Governance architecture

How mandates, accountability, legal frameworks, and public institutions organise responsibility for displaced populations.

Fiscal systems

How budgets, development finance, and recurrent public obligations shape state absorption capacity.

Sustainable inclusion

How health, education, registration, and social protection systems can absorb displacement-related vulnerability.

Regional mapping

Comparative analysis of where governance systems break down and where institutional absorption is possible.

Origins

The platform emerged from work on protection, internal displacement, humanitarian coordination, and institutional reform.

Displacement Policy was established by Samuel Cheung, whose work spans humanitarian operations, policy development, institutional reform, and international protection.

Over two decades with UNHCR, Samuel served in headquarters, regional, and field leadership roles covering refugee protection, internal displacement, mixed migration, durable solutions, and humanitarian coordination.

His experience includes serving as Chief of Internal Displacement, Chief of Protection from Violence and Displacement, and Global Protection Cluster Coordinator. These experiences inform the platform's focus on how institutions organise responsibility for displacement, how public systems adapt to displacement-related vulnerability, and how sustainable responses are financed and maintained over time.

Looking Forward

As displacement becomes an increasingly recurrent public challenge, questions of governance become more important.

How is responsibility allocated across institutions? How do public systems absorb displacement-related vulnerability? How are long-term obligations financed? And how can protection, inclusion, and durable solutions be sustained through ordinary systems rather than exceptional responses?

Displacement Policy is intended as a space for research, analysis, reflection, and debate on these questions.
