Platform

How public systems respond when displacement becomes a recurrent governance challenge.

Displacement Policy examines how states, institutions, and public systems organise responses to displacement across humanitarian, development, and climate contexts. Its focus is not only on displacement itself, but on the governance arrangements through which responsibility for displaced populations is allocated — across laws, institutions, public systems, and financing structures.

The platform brings together conversations that are routinely treated as separate: refugee protection, internal displacement, migration, humanitarian response, disaster risk management, development finance, social protection, and climate adaptation.

What the platform examines

Core governance questions.

  • Responsibility allocation across institutions and legal frameworks
  • Fiscal architecture and recurrent public obligations
  • Public systems and their capacity for sustainable inclusion
  • Institutional adaptation to displacement as a governance condition
  • Regional mapping of where systems break down and where absorption is possible

Research Focus

Current areas of work.

Governance architecture

How mandates, accountability frameworks, and legal instruments organise state responsibility for displaced populations.

Fiscal systems

How public budgets, development finance instruments, and recurrent fiscal obligations shape a state's capacity to absorb displacement-related demand.

Sustainable inclusion

How health, education, registration, and social protection systems can incorporate displacement-related vulnerability into ordinary public provision.

Regional mapping

Comparative analysis of governance arrangements across Southeast Asia and beyond, identifying where institutional absorption is feasible and where structural constraints persist.

Founder & Research Director

The platform emerged from work on protection, displacement, humanitarian coordination, and institutional reform.

Samuel Cheung is the founder and research director of Displacement Policy. He spent two decades with UNHCR in field and headquarters roles across Asia, the Middle East, and Africa, serving as Chief of Internal Displacement, Chief of Protection from Violence and Displacement, and Global Protection Cluster Coordinator. His work covered refugee protection, internal displacement, mixed migration, durable solutions, and humanitarian coordination — at country, regional, and headquarters levels.

Prior to UNHCR, he advised Asian sovereigns on bond programmes and structured finance at Allen & Overy LLP in Hong Kong. He holds a JD from Georgetown University Law Center.

These experiences inform the platform's central questions: how institutions organise responsibility for displacement, how public systems adapt to displacement-related vulnerability, and how sustainable responses are financed and maintained through recurrent rather than exceptional arrangements.

Looking Forward

As displacement becomes an increasingly recurrent public challenge, governance questions become more consequential.

How is responsibility allocated across institutions? How do public systems absorb displacement-related vulnerability without resorting to parallel or temporary provision? How are long-term obligations financed when displacement extends beyond humanitarian timescales?

Displacement Policy is a space for research, analysis, and debate on these questions — from scholars, practitioners, and policymakers engaging with displacement as an institutional problem rather than a temporary emergency.
