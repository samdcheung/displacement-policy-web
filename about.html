About Displacement Policy

Displacement Policy examines displacement through the lens of governance.

Platform

How responsibility for displacement is organised, financed, and sustained over time.

Displacement Policy explores how states, institutions, and public systems respond to displacement across humanitarian, development, and climate contexts.

Its focus is not only on displacement itself, but on the governance arrangements through which responsibility for displaced populations is allocated across laws, institutions, public systems, and financing arrangements.

Displacement is often studied through separate lenses: refugee protection, internal displacement, migration, humanitarian response, disaster risk management, development, social protection, or climate adaptation. While each provides important insights, policy and institutional responses are frequently fragmented across sectors, mandates, and time horizons.

Displacement Policy seeks to bring these conversations into closer dialogue through a governance lens. The platform examines how legal frameworks, public institutions, fiscal systems, social protection programmes, and development policies interact in shaping displacement outcomes.

Research Focus

The platform focuses on governance architecture, institutional adaptation, fiscal systems, and pathways to sustainable inclusion.

Research currently focuses on:

  • Governance architecture and institutional responsibility
  • Fiscal architecture and public finance
  • Social protection and sustainable inclusion
  • Institutional adaptation and state capacity
  • Regional and comparative approaches to displacement governance
  • Protection leadership and public policy

The platform's work includes conceptual frameworks, policy analysis, comparative governance mapping, and applied research on displacement systems in Southeast Asia and beyond.

While the platform addresses displacement governance globally, current comparative research focuses on Southeast Asia as the first regional application of its governance mapping methodology.

Origins

The platform emerged from work on protection, internal displacement, humanitarian coordination, and institutional reform.

Displacement Policy was established by Samuel Cheung, whose work spans humanitarian operations, policy development, institutional reform, and international protection.

Over two decades with UNHCR, Samuel served in headquarters, regional, and field leadership roles covering refugee protection, internal displacement, mixed migration, durable solutions, and humanitarian coordination.

His experience includes serving as Chief of Internal Displacement, Chief of Protection from Violence and Displacement, and Global Protection Cluster Coordinator. He has contributed to the development of global policy and institutional approaches relating to protection, internal displacement, humanitarian coordination, and durable solutions.

These experiences inform the platform's focus on how institutions organise responsibility for displacement, how public systems adapt to displacement-related vulnerability, and how sustainable responses are financed and maintained over time.

Looking Forward

As displacement becomes an increasingly recurrent public challenge, questions of governance become more important.

How is responsibility allocated across institutions? How do public systems absorb displacement-related vulnerability? How are long-term obligations financed? And how can protection, inclusion, and durable solutions be sustained through ordinary systems rather than exceptional responses?

Displacement Policy is intended as a space for research, analysis, reflection, and debate on these questions.
