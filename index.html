About Displacement Policy

Displacement Policy is a research platform examining displacement through the lens of governance.

The project bridges humanitarian policy, development policy, and public administration to explore how responsibility for displaced populations is organised across laws, institutions, public systems, and financing arrangements.

Its focus is not only on displacement itself, but on the capacity of states and institutions to respond, adapt, and sustain responsibility over time.

Research focuses on governance architecture, institutional adaptation, social protection, fiscal systems, and pathways to sustainable inclusion.

Mapping Project

Southeast Asia Displacement Governance Mapping

A comparative evidence initiative examining how states govern displacement through legal frameworks, institutions, public systems, and financing arrangements.

The mapping identifies governance architectures, institutional fault lines, and pathways through which displacement-related vulnerability may be absorbed into ordinary public systems.

Current regional focus: Southeast Asia.
