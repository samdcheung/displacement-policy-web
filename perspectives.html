Perspectives

Reflections, commentary, and debate on displacement governance.

Perspectives brings together short essays from Displacement Policy and invited contributors exploring how displacement is governed through institutions, public systems, financing arrangements, and political processes.

The section begins with a series of reflections by Samuel Cheung drawing on experience in global policy development, protection leadership, internal displacement, and humanitarian coordination. Future contributions will include perspectives from policymakers, practitioners, researchers, and affected communities.

Founding Reflections

Governance & Institutions

From Protection Leadership to Governance Leadership

Reflecting on Protection Leadership: The Role of UNHCR in Humanitarian Crises

When Protection Leadership was developed, the central question was how humanitarian actors could exercise leadership for protection in increasingly complex crises. A decade later, the challenge looks different. As displacement becomes more protracted and responsibility shifts toward national systems, the question is increasingly one of governance rather than humanitarian leadership alone.

Samuel Cheung

Protection & Governance

Beyond the IASC Protection Policy

Reflections on protection, accountability, and institutional responsibility

The revised IASC Policy on Protection of Internally Displaced Persons sought to strengthen collective accountability for protection outcomes. Yet implementation continues to reveal a deeper challenge: policies can establish responsibilities, but institutions determine whether those responsibilities are carried in practice.

Samuel Cheung

Guest Perspectives

Perspectives also publishes invited contributions from policymakers, practitioners, researchers, civil society leaders, and affected communities.

Contributors are invited to reflect on emerging challenges in displacement governance, including protection, internal displacement, climate mobility, social protection, development finance, urbanisation, and institutional reform.

Future contributions will include perspectives from international organisations, governments, academic institutions, civil society organisations, and regional policy leaders.

Contribute

Displacement Policy welcomes concise analytical essays, reflections, and commentaries.

Typical contributions range from 500-1,500 words and should focus on ideas, institutions, policy choices, and governance challenges rather than organisational positions.
